How to make Limber de Coco

I made Coconut Limbers (as I used to call them) with my Dad when I was younger. This was one of my first introductions to cooking. I was so fascinated by making them and this memory has stood with me over time. The following recipe comes from this New York Times article by Millie Peachtree Peartree.

Ingredients

  • 1 Cup half-and-half
  • 1(12-ounce) can sweetened, condensed coconut milk
  • 1 (13-ounce) can full-fat coconut milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Alternative Ingredients:
    • Evaporated Milk
    • Cream of Coconut
    • Cream of Coconut
    • Sweetened Condensed Milk

Directions

  1. In a large bowl or spouted measuring cup, blend or whisk the half-and-half into the sweetened, condensed coconut milk. Add the remaining ingredients and 1/2 cup water and whisk to combine. Pour into small disposable cups and freeze for at least 4 hours, until frozen.
  2. To serve, squeeze the cup to push the limber up, and eat it directly from the cup. You could also pull it out of the cup and flip it, so the base becomes the top, and eat.
